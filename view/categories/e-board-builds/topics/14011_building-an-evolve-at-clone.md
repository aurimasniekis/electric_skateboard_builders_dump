I'd stick with the Trampa trucks. The sidewinders are a bit sketch at high speeds. For decks, it's better to have something more stiff than a dancer. ## \#41 Posted by: Mobutusan Posted at: 2016-12-08T17:21:56.509Z Reads: 256

```
Since @Okami mentioned it, here's my build thread. 

And does anyone make spring/channel trucks with 10mm axles?

http://www.electric-skateboard.builders/t/uncharted-territory-20-hyline-mtn-board-earthboard-250mm-trucks-8-200mm-wheels-8s-12s-dual-12s-tb-escs-korryh-motor-mounts-kaly-mtb-pulleys-dual-245kv-6364-mad-munkey/8041

I did source some motor mounts from @korryh and mounted them on round trucks using the caliber clamp and some strategic filing. I think all the info should be in my thread, but short answer is that it is working very well so far. Also of note, is that he was able to make me an extended-length motor plate that had 2 sets of mounting slots to fit motor/pulleys/belts for mountain board or a  street board set up. Although it seems easiest to just swap the wheel/pulley/belt with a matching street wheel/pulley/belt and 10mm bearings. 

One thing I haven't seen mentioned much is axle size and street wheel compatibility. From what I've learned, and anyone correct me if I'm wrong, 8mm and 10mm axles will allow you to easily run AT or Street Wheels as long as you have the proper size 22mm x 7mm x 10mm I.D. bearings. It is when you get into the 12 millimeter axles and corresponding wheels that the bearing size changes to 28mm x 8mm x 12mm, and won't fit into regular street wheels. 

So, if I were in your position and trying to save money, I would try to pick up a used MBS board or something that has extra wide skate trucks with 10mm axles. Then you'd have the trucks and AT wheels covered, and could still fit your street wheels with a set of 10mm bearings. Keeping the mountain board deck or not would be up to you. I'd get my pulleys/belts from @kaly, motor mounts from @kaly or @korryh, 3D print @juniorpotato93's 15mm flywheel pulleys, buy 10mm skate bearings from eBay, and get some extra belts from sdp/si to fit your street wheel/pulley setup. Then you should be able to easily swap between wheels. And you'd have some foot straps too, if you wanted to use them!
```
