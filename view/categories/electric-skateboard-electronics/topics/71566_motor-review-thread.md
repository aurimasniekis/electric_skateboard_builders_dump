## \#21 Posted by: Jmding Posted at: 2018-10-17T19:44:30.371Z Reads: 275

```
1) magnet problems
2) shaft problems
3) overheating (current limits set too high?)
4) bearing quality
5) waterproofing (sensored only)

Good list of qualities to look for, but unfortunately there are challenges.  With 1, 2, and 4, there is little that the buyer can do to identify motors that will exhibit these issues.  With 3, it is likely user misuse, excessively high motor currents set in VESC configurations because retailers are not properly communicating that lower Kv motors have lower current limits.  5 is a valid concern, and easy to check as well, but probably relatively minor in terms of significance.

So in the end we're still left with mostly hearsay and sporadic reports of motor failure.  I can't say with confidence whether an SK3 or a Maytech has stronger magnets, better retaining epoxy, or higher quality bearings.
```

## \#56 Posted by: lrdesigns Posted at: 2018-10-18T04:30:25.657Z Reads: 197

```
This is my experience too with destroying many brushless motors in RC models. Voltage max is irrelevant so long as you don't exceed the load capacity of the motor. Max Amps on the other hand is an issue too many amps and you melt the insulation on the windings. Amps is also proportional to the load put on the motor. Choose the wrong gear ratio or propeller size and you can kill a motor in a few seconds from overheating. On the other side if the motor is under loaded you can run much higher voltage without issue, until the g-force of the rpm starts to rip the motor apart. Which you would never reach in an e-skate application. 

Also to hummies point the specs from manufactures are impossible to trust and every one of them seems to use a different method to determine max current / voltage / watts.  Watts is really the most important one as heat handling is the critical spec.
```

There are already quite a few suitable motors now.  The problem is that no matter what motor you use there will always be someone who has had an issue.  Use the tried and tested units that people have been using for years and you should be fine. 
For example;  I have 4 sk8 192's  Out of the four 1 threw a bearing.  After 50 km of use.  Would I recommend them to someone else?  Of course I would, it was an anomaly.  It was replaced and all good. 
I have 2 maytechs which have been fine for a year.  Would I recommend them?  Yes of course I would.
I have 2 sk3 which are my personal favourite and obviously I would recommend them 
I have 2 tb 6374's that are fine after 6 months and again recommended.
I have 3 cheap generic chinese oem motors that have all failed.  I bought them because they were **cheap** and no I wouldn't recommend them to anyone.
Point is no motor or indeed anything we use is going to be bullet proof (that's the holy grail) but you get what you pay for and you pay for good quality control and decent customer service for when that QC lets you down.
