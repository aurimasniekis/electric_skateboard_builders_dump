## \#30 Posted by: barajabali Posted at: 2018-01-05T17:09:07.525Z Reads: 88

```
Put glue in this hole but dont push it in too far, just enough to the little knob on the end can glide over the arch, giving you the circular motion. 

<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/f/2/f2a77f31148b53274029447c3357918ea51c5281.jpg" width="375" height="500">


I also took this time to cut off the mirroring button. I dont need it personally. 
<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/2/4/2451ed005429d5c7f691f4c501a7fa743de47371.jpg" width="375" height="500">

I also glued the throttle module to these three ridges, there was a little bit of play. Make sure its not on the center or you will lock your throttle up.
<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/2/4/243d238d59b3bd408a4cef85c689cab9a579a264.jpg" width="375" height="500">
Here you can see how it looks after
<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/3/8/386f477ce9429d6cd0f4205ddcdd3e24e73cb887.jpg" width="666" height="500">
I also glued the USB port for added support, and I glued all solder connections that I saw. 
<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/f/a/fa331aaf42fa06b4e5567f733d583c4b364316f8.jpg" width="666" height="500">


Additionally, you can coat this remotes electronics with conformal coating or corrosion x to water proof. I dont ride my r2 in the rain or snow so I am not going to do that. 
End result: no slop, super sturdy and I dont have to worry about my throttle reversing when i'm holding on to my controller and the ridiculous speed of the r2.

HAPPY MODDING!
```
