## \#182 Posted by: Jinra Posted at: 2016-08-04T15:41:48.770Z Reads: 129

```
So due to the dense nature of the components in the space cell pro 4 while running dual VESCs, careful consideration needs to be taken. I've had a bunch of signal issues due to EMI, or high current noise. This caused my remote to cut out a couple times and made me eat it. 

I've also noticed that sometimes my slave VESC would fail to respond during and immediately after high current riding. This resulted in reduced torque and braking ability. It also caused the slave VESC to respond in a laggy manner. I would throttle and the master VESC would respond, but the slave VESC takes the command 1 second later or not at all. Sometimes this would mean my board is throttling forward when my finger is off the controller. I believe this is due to  EMI creating noise in the CAN bus cable. 

If you're considering running a similar setup (dual VESC) I'd highly consider investing in some ferrite rings to reduce the noise on the CAN bus and receiver cables. I currently have a large ring (posted above) that has both cables going through it (not shown in picture). I have tested this momentarily and my problems seem to be resolved but it is definitely too early to confirm. I will ride more this weekend to test it out.

@t1m0007
```
