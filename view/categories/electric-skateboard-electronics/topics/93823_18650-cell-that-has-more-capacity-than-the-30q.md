```
Haha, pushing them abit passed what theyre intended for wont cause flames ;) 
If you run a belted setup with good gear ratio, you have alot of margin in regards to current.
In such an application, keeping a constant high drain for a longer period is seldom seen. Only way is to climb a really steep hill. Unless you have these hills. Sure, youll draw alot of current on startup maybe 50A and higher. But that top peak only lasts for so long. Keeping a constant speed doesnt consume an awful lot, something most people seem to forget. Sure margins are good. But if you draw 80A for 3 seconds on a pack that only should do 40-50 amps cont., nothing bad will happen(again, temp plays part here). You might cause more ware on your cells, but thats it.
```
