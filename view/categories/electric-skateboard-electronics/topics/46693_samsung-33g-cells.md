## \#61 Posted by: xilw3r Posted at: 2018-02-18T13:16:10.285Z Reads: 75

```
At 5p with those 33G cells you have 33A "rated" current, but you WILL kill those cells in a month or two, they will loose performance like crazy. Same goes for the Panasonic PF cells @getyorma  mentioned if you are going to use them close to the "rated" limit, they will suffer, 8p is great, 80A peaks, but still, with 2 motors I'd say its close to the limit of PF's.

You must have overhead with li-ion cells. I am using VTC6 cells in 4p for my battery, which is "theoretically rated" for 120A, with a smal catch- the batteries MUST not reach 80 degrees C. Means a cooled pack is required. In real world testing even the mighty VTC6 can only do 15A continous without cooling (so my pack becomes rated for 60A, just about right with ~30% headroom)

Now about safety... I will not go into details of what Li-po cells are made of, its too deep. But i will say this:

18650 cells that come from a trusted manufacturer are MUCH safer than any damned Li-po you can find. they will NOT blow up. But they can catch fire under some circumstances. A "nice" example - a guy is spot welding some cells, cranks up the welder to max for some dumb reason and forgets- the electrode punctures the cell from the top shorting the anode and cathode- a literal fountain of chemial fire appears. Bad move. Smillarly if a large pack is shorted it can catch fire.

To prevent bad situations one thing people should do is build battery packs where each cell has individual fuses. It is the thing that can really prevent a flaming battery

But all in all you can do really a lot of damage to 18650's without having anything bad happen. All thanks to the quite a few sophisticated safety mechanisms in the cell, a good picture posted by @bevilacqua (but one must keep in mind that cheap chinese "ultrafire" level batteries dont have many, if any of those. short an ultrafire and it will explode on you. In a while. Same for damaging or shorting a Li-po... No, please don't. Its literally a bomb. Mainly because no standards, no really trusted and safe constructions.. its all a black dangerous chemical box.
```

## \#74 Posted by: PXSS Posted at: 2018-02-18T16:33:55.573Z Reads: 68

```
I've specifically tested shorting out battery packs. It's one of the airforce requirements. When shorted, the pattern is very much random.

This did not happen during regular use. It was a test specifically made to see how the battery would react to an outside short without protection from the BMS.

NASA has performed similar tests with similar results. They're trying to improve the reliability of the CIDs and bottom of the cell venting to decrease side wall rupturing. I believe they had something like a 30% side wall rupturing in their testing. 

Plus, the CID venting is only safe if the vent is not pointed directly at another cell. That's why in most applications cells aren't stacked on top of each other without something between them. Have you seen how esk8 packs are assembled? If a cell CID blows, you have at best a 50% chance of it venting away from the rest of the pack. If it vents directly into another cell, then you just made things worse.
```
