```
That's not that tall for a top mount... That's actually about average.

Decreasing your grip tape level is just going to decrease the distance of leverage (top of deck to pivot point)

Less leverage = less twitchy, but harder to lean into turns due to the decrease in leverage.

I would stick to the suggestion of others
- dewedge rear
- bushing changes
- cup changes.

You should be able to tune that board fairly easily.

If you really are set on dropping it... I would mill baseplate cutouts to sink the trucks farther into your AIO deck..but that may result in wheelbite and the need to increase the size of your wheelwells
```

## \#56 Posted by: Alphamail Posted at: 2018-09-15T17:59:52.822Z Reads: 93

```
Fyre trucks are very different from Ronin trucks in that they remove all the load bearing responsibility from the bushing.  This opens up a lot of possibilities to run much softer bushings than you normally would or could. You can actually ride them without bushings or just use a roadside bushing.  Not recommended but it can be done.  They can also automatically adjust to various height bushings within a reasonable range.  We make both the front and rear pivots for them which makes them even better and a lot quieter.  Really an amazing truck for the price although availability can be an issue if you want something other than common base plate angles or hanger widths.
```

## \#72 Posted by: scrapheap Posted at: 2018-09-17T12:41:44.450Z Reads: 73

```
I've only had experience with Ronins, Liquids, and Ventures, so I can't give a fair comparison. I can only give my personal opinion and experiences on why I like the Fyres more than Cast Ronins.

Both trucks are quite stable, but the Fyres feel more lively to me. Cast Ronins felt sort of robotic to me and I didn't have much fun carving with them as I did the Fyres. And Ronins weren't really designed for decks with really short wheelbases...so that also had an effect, since I had planned this build as a mini build. That and the added hangar length of the Fyres help add a little more stability on a short deck vs the Ronins.

Both are really good trucks though, but adjusting bushings, washers, and baseplate angles have an impact on the stability and feel of the ride too! (big thanks to @alphamail for helping me out with split-angle washer adjustments!).

Only frustrating part is that no one makes truck mounts for the Liquids (because they aren't common), so you have to buy whatever is on the market and modify them to work, if you don't resort to making one yourself. I had to use a buttload of sugru + silicone to get Etoxx's Ronin mounts to work, and @Namasaki filed Torqueboards' mounts a little bit to work for him.
```
