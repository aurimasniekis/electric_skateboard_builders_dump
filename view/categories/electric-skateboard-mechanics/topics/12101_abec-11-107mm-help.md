```
I seem to find the wheels do come up from time to time. Stores don't seem to stock to many sets at one time. One store told me he had a set that sat unsold for months. 
I'm currently working on my second build with these wheels. My first build I've used Torqueboard 15mm wheel pulley and motor mount. Unfortunately, he doesn't sell either of them any more. Because of the off set of the wheel, the mount has to move inward quite a bit. For the wheel pulley I had a spacer made up so that the pulley sat flush with the wheel.

For my second build, I ended up using trucks from Psychotiller and a custom motor mount. The mount is similar to TB newest mount. I needed something custom to mount the 8072 motor. Also a wheel spacer has to be made up. 
Either way there is really no pulley or motor mount that will work without some mods. I'm sure you are aware that there are printable wheels pulleys that you can get made.

## \#15 Posted by: cdn Posted at: 2016-10-30T17:57:12.262Z Reads: 134

```
Everything had to be custom made to make use of these wheels. The motor mount is identical in design with TB mount, other than its size being larger. The larger mount was needed for the larger motor. The mount is made from 7075 grade aluminum. It was made the DIY way using a Dremel, drill, belt sander, tap and die set. Both the wheel pulley and motor pulley were sourced from Ebay. The wheel pulley did have to be modified in order for it to work. The motor pulley needed no mods. Pulley  ratio 40:24. 

There was some modification done to the trucks to make the mount work. The little triangle portion on the truck had to be removed. The mounts open portion where it clamps around the truck sits on part of the truck casting that allows for it to not rotate when clamped down. Hope that makes sense. 

I'm really curious to see how this larger motor works out. Its rated at 4200W. Visually I think this motor pairs well with the Abec 107's. As long as you are located within the US we can set you up with a FreeRide with 107's but we may need a little more time before delivery due to changes in the gear ratio. Need to reduce the the drive pulley motor side to accommodate the larger circumference of the wheels.

Expect a full release of specs and options within 2 weeks. 

A few things to considering when choosing our drive.

1. Our drive is designed specifically for our motors, not all motors will fit. In time we will have a more universal design but we are focused on this specific dual motor configuration at this time.

2. Mounts only fit trucks machined to match the hanger diameter of our drive trains,  trucks need to machined to match and will be available separately and in a package form. There is no "universal" sleeve. In our experience this is the only way to have a solid reliable drive train.
