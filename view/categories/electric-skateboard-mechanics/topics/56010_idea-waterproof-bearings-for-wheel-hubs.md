## \#8 Posted by: CHAINMAILLEKID Posted at: 2018-05-19T15:38:51.257Z Reads: 199

```
Ok, so I've had this idea for a while... Its a little off the wall.

Hollow axles hooked up to an air pump which port air into the core of your wheels, and the positive pressure keeps water and dust from entering your bearings.

As far as the slip ring idea, its actually really easy to seal up the wheels on the outside.

So this is a dumb old picture, but I was playing around few years ago with caps that happened to fit on wheels.![IMG_2999|666x500](upload://n2H3qQ1RALBfK50AdUaKxg8yhCb.JPG)
I started with caps, but I ended up just capping wheels off with duct tape before I ended up dropping the idea.

But basically, you don't have to worry about a slip ring at all on the outside, because you can just bridge over the whole nut and bearing completely. 

So your idea would only need to apply to the inside bearing.

I'm also going to rep thick waterproof grease again.
Its really hard for rust to settle in on metal thats got a coating of oil or grease on it. But skate oils just do not hold up and have high wash out when they get wet, which leave with wet bearings that have lost their protective film of oil that prevents rust.

Schaeffers 274 is the bomb.
https://www.amazon.com/Schaeffer-Manufacturing-02742-029S-Synthetic-Grease/dp/B00JF2LBQ6
This stuff is more waterproof than you can believe.

And I'll also repeat myself and say there is no reason not to use thick grease on Eskate. You're no longer relying on only gravity for acceleration, you've got the power of a motor and the tiny bit of rolling drag caused by grease becomes almost entirely irrelevant.
```

## \#13 Posted by: b264 Posted at: 2018-05-19T20:10:03.054Z Reads: 159

```
[quote="anorak234, post:11, topic:56010"]
Aren‚Äôt ceramic bearings waterproof?
[/quote]

Yes, "full ceramic" are.  Those have ceramic balls and ceramic races.  Regular "ceramic" bearings have steel races and ceramic balls and are not waterproof.  There is a also a difference between "waterproof" (designed to work with water ingress) and "water resistant" (designed to prevent water ingress, but may be damaged by it)
```
