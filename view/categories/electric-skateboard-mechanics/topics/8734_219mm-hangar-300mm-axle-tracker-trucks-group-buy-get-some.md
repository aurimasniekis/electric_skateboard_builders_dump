## \#49 Posted by: whitepony Posted at: 2016-09-05T04:53:21.360Z Reads: 216

```
just chiming in - unless you want to use crazy tall dual motors or dual 15mm or all terrain wheels, I would always go for a truck thats is narrow as possible because of grip. 

im not a big fan of boosted and evolve track width at all, because you have noticeably less grip with regular longboard wheels on wide trucks (pneumatics are a different story, they will never slip ever really).

can easily explain the behavior - if your truck had a hangar width of 0, youd run a mono wheel and no matter how hard you lean in: youd always have pretty much the same load and the full grip of the 2 wheels. now you bring the wheels apart and the further you do, the lesser the grip on the outer wheel and the more the inner wheel is overloaded when you are leaning into a turn. 
single strong motor builds will know that phenomenon when the motor driven wheel is outwards, it might break loose. so, carving-hungry single motor users like me should actually look for narrower trucks than the typical ones for improved traction! :sunglasses:
```
