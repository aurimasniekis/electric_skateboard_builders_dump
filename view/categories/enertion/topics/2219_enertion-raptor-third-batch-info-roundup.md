## \#1 Posted by: treeme Posted at: 2016-04-08T04:56:33.659Z Reads: 524

```
Hi All,

Long time lurker, first time poster. I wanted to start this new thread to communally discuss the next batch of raptors that are coming out. I want to use this as a platform to round up all the information that has been posted on different threads about the expectations for the raptor Such as shipping dates, added features, etc.

Here is what I've found through previous threads as well as pms with @onloop:

Added features:

 - Pure carbon fiber lids have been replaced with Fiberglass lids. They have the same carbon fiber pattern
 - VESC setting modifications:
- custom ppm settings 
- soft limit set in place to prevent unplanned shutdown from high current draw on a low battery
- potential modifications to motor current
- potential modification to brake strength
 - Better QA on bolts
- cause: first batch had some liquid preventing tight bolting on the thread locker
 - Push button replaced with toggle switch on battery
 - Injection molded wheel pulley (thanks, @Adam0311)
 - Foam gasket to reduce vibration (thanks, @onloop)
 - 6 screws to hold lid down (thanks, @onloop) 

Eventual improvements:

 - Phone app for changing VESC settings w/ presets
- seems to be underway through @jacobbloy (potentially independent work but metes same end goal)
 - IP68 electronics enclosure
 - Battery option w/ 33% greater capacity
- solved (?) with new space cell pro 4 batteries

Questions left to be determined

 - Expected ship date for next batch
- CF decks didn't arrive until around April 1st, 2016
- "Nearly all the outstanding orders will ship this month, there is a chance some of the very recent orders won't have a vesc to go inside them until the next VESC batch arrives" - @onloop  
 - what order #s will be included in next batch?

Updates suggested by current Raptor owners:

 - Shorter bolts for enclosure to reduce time spent screwing/unscrewing

Shipping Plan:

* Orders <b>before February 27th</b>:
- Shipment should be getting out the week of <b>April 17-April 23</b>. My order feel in this group but has not shipped yet. 
- <b>On February 27th</b>: Some will ship the week of <b>April 17- April 23</b>, some <b>by May 4th</b>
- <b>After March 13th</b>: Waiting on VESCs, could be about <b>4 weeks</b>, still unclear.

Status of raptors (as per vlog post on [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I_rb1-v4s0E)):

* 30 raptor decks in house, construction has begun
- another 10 decks should be coming in next week ( week of April 17, 2016 )
- Wheels came in mid may

Status of raptors as of April 20th (as per post on [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ues97QGzYRU)

* Shipment dates were clarified (see above).

<b>As of May 11th, Shipments have begun for the next batch.</b>
<b>As of May 23rd, VESCs have started coming in to be completed. Shipments for orders that need VESCs will shortly resume</b>
```

Imagine the throttle is on a scale from 0 to 10, where 5 is neutral, 0 is all the way back, 10 is full forward.

When going forward, I have the throttle at 7 (somewhat forward). Then, I start going downhill, so I immediately let go of the throttle.

## \#426 Posted by: Maxid Posted at: 2016-06-09T10:09:49.612Z Reads: 105

```
[quote="HH1, post:425, topic:2219, full:true"]
...but would you just have the same problem with 6s?
[/quote]

not as far as I know, as the voltage is mainly limiting the top speed. At lower voltage you can have the same energy output as with high voltage - all you need is higher current.
The thing that is controlled by the VESC is the current while the voltage stays nearly constant (3.X to 4.2V per cell). So with reduced voltage the VESC can just use higher current to compensate. At higher voltage and limited current draw the VESC can only go up to the (in this case relatively low) current limit.

Think of it like:
Volt: How fast the motor can turn (top speed)
Ampere: How quickly can you get the motor to turn that fast (acceleration)
Top speed stays nearly constant while you control the acceleration with your throttle.
```

## \#685 Posted by: Photorph Posted at: 2016-07-19T06:50:24.721Z Reads: 97

```
The bottom of the enclosure has a double sided velcro.  here is what I would do step by step.

1) Don't mess with the double sided velcro.  Put the battery in and make sure it fits well.  Don't need to plug in battery yet, just see how you will plug it in and where you will lay the wires.  There should be enough space to lay out the wires w/o putting too many crimps in them.

2) Take the battery back out and peel the sticker off the velcro and then just put the battery in.  Plug in battery.  Push the wires in the small amounts of open space that are there around the motor controllers.  

3) Put the lid on and screw in all the screw they gave you.  That's it!

The controller will automatically sync with it.  Turn the board on, hold the top front button until it turns on.  The light should be red when it is connected to the VESC.  Be careful and have the raptor laying upside down, so you don't smash it into something accidentally.  The joystick is very light.  Check to make sure it accelerates and brakes.
```
