```
The closer to nominal voltage (or about 40-50% on a meter) the better it is for the cell. This includes for charging. ## \#188 Posted by: Jinra Posted at: 2016-08-16T04:53:32.222Z Reads: 89
```

```
You can check this discharge curve too

<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/2X/7/7cb8da4da54564178b2b2b6d30b4d0c18f97711e.png" width="657" height="413">

Take a look at the 10-15A discharge curve. Once you use about 1.25 amp hour (50%) of the 2.5amp hours in a 18650 cell you have about 3.4v on the cell. However, due to voltage sag this is probably closer to 3.6v. Since the graph is non-linear the cells deplete rapid when voltage falls even lower, so it wouldn't read 50% on a voltmeter.

That said, 3.6 is about 50% of the capacity of the battery, but won't necessarily read 50% on a voltmeter. You should still have a bit of charge after nominal voltage though, more than 5%.
```
