```
Ask and you shall receive. :slight_smile: Just a note though, these are the v1 mounts. I haven't taken a look at how the v2 mounts work, but I know they're different in the aspect that they aren't a spring loading, self tensioning system anymore. 

The mounts themselves are held on by a type of glue. Not very efficient seeing as loose motor mounts was one of the v1 owners' pain points:

<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/f/7/f7d2f2448c00fc8bacd1a393d23169fb35cc4511.jpg" width="375" height="500">

<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/4/f/4fdc2132d20d499b31cd888875b245aa9af96899.jpg" width="375" height="500">

Here you can see the grooves in the mount for where the springs sat. The motor slides into the mount and compresses the springs. When a belt is attached between the pulleys, the springs would self tension the belt. This was made possible by some bolts that went through the encoder cap through to the motor through those holes (I think... it's been awhile):

<img src="/uploads/db1493/original/3X/7/f/7fc633ac3825b11481f1fc270eeea89261ca84ca.jpg" width="375" height="500">

## \#122 Posted by: trampa Posted at: 2017-03-31T07:49:56.686Z Reads: 317

```
Hi, using these mounts you want a long motor shaft (min. 38.5mm outside motor) and use the support bearing. You can flip the caliber shaped disc, so that the panels are just in the right angle towards the deck/truck. Making long motor shafts is not to much of a job. We use 15mm Belts to prevent belt slip. Wide belts make all the difference. @Gromok: the flat spot on the Carbon Panles faces towards the ground to prevent the carbon from splitting when touching the pavement. It works the other way around but you could ruin the Carbon. Depending on your motor size (diameter), you could also reverse the carbon panel to stick out in the back of the board. Just reverse the caliber shaped disc and the carbon panel.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yWsmGnCjm4s

Frank
